Music

Disco is a genre of music that became popular in the United States in the 1970s. Disco was known for its upbeat catchy tempo played by electric pianos, synthesizers (a type of piano) and guitars. The vocals are also known to be repetitive. Artists that have made an influence in the Disco world were singers such as Donna Summer, Bees Gees, Gloria Gaynor, and Kool & the Gang. Although Disco did not last long, artists were able to leave a legacy.

History

Disco is known to come from the 1970s, but did you know that it wasn’t popular until the late 70s and early 80s? Disco was slowly introduced in the early 70s but didn’t take a boom until the movie “Saturday Night Fever” with John Travolta premiered. After the premiere, disco was at its peak in popularity. Though the popularity of Disco declined, other genres of music were created that were inspired by disco such as House Music, DJ Culture and early Hip-Hop.
